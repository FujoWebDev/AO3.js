There was a reason he smiled when he realized he died.

Because that was the one moment he felt real.

Really real.

It was as if his whole life, he hadn't known what it was like. To feel, to breathe somehow, even as he was suffocating.

His whole life he had never really felt anything.

He was STRONG. He could do many things while being STRONG. He never got the chance to be anything but STRONG. Even Nepeta, the best friend and moriail he could ever ask for, couldn't quite understand what it was like, not being able to hold something close without it breaking from the contact.

That's why he had forced himself to be obsessed with blood color.
It had started out as a simple thing, something he could hold onto without it breaking. But it became his everything as he got stronger; destroying robots with a touch, even harming his lustus in an attempts to be kind. He decided that it had to stop before he ended up killing something or someone. So he grasped onto what he could.

It gave him a reason to be worthless. He was of a lesser blood; that's why he couldn't hold what he loved! He was a lesser blood, he had a reason to feel worthless, a reason to be a complete screw-up that he knew he was.

Nepeta could see it, but she didn't mention it. Equius knew that she knew; if it was ever brought up he pushed it away.
He didn't need anything to change his mind. He knew what he was, an absolute monster of the world. Even the mutant-blood-rogue was better than him. At least HE could hold what he wanted close.

Equius didn't even get that.

He didn't even get the chance to hold something close to him and say goodbye one last time. Not once chance to protect something without hurting it. He couldn't do it for Aradia, he couldn't do it for Nepeta, and he couldn't do it for himself. Everything he did, it was a joke from the start.

So when the fantastic purple blood strung the rope around his neck, he didn't resist.

He knew he should have. He knew somehow Nepeta would find out and then she'd end up dying too.

But right then, when he felt PAIN for the first and the last time...
...He felt like he really hadn't any regrets.

Suffocation- to overcome or extinguish; suppress.

He extinguished his worst enemy

Himself.
